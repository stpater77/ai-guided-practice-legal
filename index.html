FAQ

Common questions

Do I need any coding experience?
Absolutely not. If you can use a web browser and type an email, you can do everything I teach. That's not a marketing line — it's the truth. Every workflow uses no-code tools or simple AI interfaces.
Is my client data safe?
Confidentiality is non-negotiable in legal. I'll show you which tools have enterprise-grade security, which ones are safe for client work, and which to avoid. Every workflow we build is designed around attorney-client privilege and data protection from the start — not as an afterthought.
What about AI hallucinations in legal research?
This is exactly why generic AI courses don't work for lawyers. I teach you how to verify outputs, build verification workflows, and use AI as a first-draft tool (not a final authority). You always review before anything leaves your desk — just like with a junior associate's work product.
Is using AI for legal work ethically permissible?
Yes — and most state bars have now issued guidance confirming it. The key is using it responsibly: understanding the tools, supervising the output, and maintaining your professional judgment. I'll walk you through the current ABA and state-specific ethics guidance so you can use AI with confidence.
How is this different from a $29 Udemy course?
Udemy courses teach you about AI. I teach you how to use AI in YOUR practice. We work on your actual documents, your actual workflows, your actual cases. You leave every session with something that works — not just knowledge, but a system your firm can use tomorrow.
What if I'm already using some AI tools?
Great — we'll audit what you're using, optimize your prompts and workflows, and identify gaps. Most attorneys I work with are using ChatGPT for basic tasks but have no idea what else is possible. That's where the real time savings are — in the workflows, not the individual prompts.
What practice areas do you cover?
The fundamentals apply across all practice areas. I've worked with attorneys in corporate, litigation, family, real estate, IP, employment, and criminal defense. The AI tools are the same — what changes is how we apply them to your specific documents, research needs, and client communication.
